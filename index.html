Ten Commandments of Computer Ethics

Thou shalt not use a computer to harm other people

Do not use computers to harm or inflict damage, whether physically, emotionally, or financially, on others.

Thou shalt not interfere with other people's computer work

Respect the work and privacy of others by not interfering with their use of computer systems.

Thou shalt not snoop around in other people's computer files

Respect the privacy of others by not accessing their files without permission.

Thou shalt not use a computer to steal

Never use a computer to steal information, property, or intellectual output from others.

Thou shalt not use a computer to bear false witness

Avoid spreading false information or rumors through any technological platform.

Thou shalt not copy or use proprietary software for which you have not paid

Respect the rights of software developers by only using licensed and paid software.

Thou shalt not use other people's computer resources without authorization or proper compensation

Always seek permission before using someone else's computer resources and offer compensation if required.

Thou shalt not appropriate other people's intellectual output

Respect intellectual property and avoid copying, using, or distributing it without permission.

Thou shalt think about the social consequences of the program you are writing or the system you are designing

Consider the social implications and potential impact of the software you develop or the system you design.

Thou shalt always use a computer in ways that ensure consideration and respect for your fellow humans

Use technology with respect, promoting positive ethical standards in your interactions.
